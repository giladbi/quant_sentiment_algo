NEW YORK (MarketWatch) — Among the companies whose shares are likely to see active trade in Monday’s session are BlackBerry Ltd., CME Group Inc., and Kellogg Co.

/quotes/zigman/19622165/delayed/quotes/nls/bbry BBRY 6.56, +0.06, +0.85%
BlackBerry Ltd.,

BlackBerry /quotes/zigman/19622165/delayed/quotes/nls/bbry BBRY +0.85%  shares were down 18% in Monday’s premarket trade after the smartphone maker said it had abandoned attempts to find a buyer.

Instead, the company will raise $1 billion from Fairfax Financial Holdings Limited and other investors through privately placed convertible debentures. Fairfax had been trying to line up the financing to take the company private.

Alongside the financing, BlackBerry announced that John Chen would take over as interim chief executive of the company, replacing CEO Thorsten Heins. Chen had been chairman and CEO of Sybase Inc. Prem Watsa, chairman and CEO of Fairfax, will be BlackBerry’s lead director and chair of the compensation, nomination and governance committee.

CME /quotes/zigman/107063/delayed/quotes/nls/cme CME +4.22%  reported third-quarter profit rose 8.6% after the exchange operator’s trading volume improved by 11%. The company reported earnings per share of 71 cents, or 75 cents per share when tax and foreign-exchange costs were excluded.

Kellogg /quotes/zigman/231157/delayed/quotes/nls/k K +0.18%  unveiled a plan Monday to trim its workforce by 7% over the next four years. The cereal-maker also announced a 2.5% increase in third-quarter earnings. Analysts at Deutsche Bank on Tuesday reiterated the stock’s buy rating, noting its cost savings are on track and its 3% dividend yield.

Tenet Healthcare Corp. /quotes/zigman/12231717/delayed/quotes/nls/thc THC +1.49%  is likely to post earnings of 46 cents a share in the third quarter after the market close on Monday. The health-care services company recently completed its purchase of Vanguard Health Systems Inc. for about $4.3 billion.
