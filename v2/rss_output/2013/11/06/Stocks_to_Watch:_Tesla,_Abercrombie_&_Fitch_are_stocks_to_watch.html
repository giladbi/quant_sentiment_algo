By Sue Chang, MarketWatch

SAN FRANCISCO (MarketWatch) — Among the companies whose shares are expected to see active trade in Wednesday’s session are Tesla Motors Inc., Abercrombie & Fitch Co., and Humana Inc.

After Tuesday’s closing bell, Tesla /quotes/zigman/118681/delayed/quotes/nls/tsla TSLA -14.51%  said it narrowed its third-quarter loss to 32 cents a share from a loss of $1.05 a share a year ago. On an adjusted basis, the electric vehicle company earned 12 cents a share. Revenue for the quarter jumped to $431.3 million from $50.1 million a year ago. But shares sank more than 9% in after hours as investors were disappointed with news that Tesla delivered only about 5,500 Model S units in the quarter. The deliveries were above the company’s own projections but below some analysts’s estimates. Craig Irwin at Wedbush had projected Tesla to deliver as many as 5,850.

Meanwhile, the company plans to deliver slightly under 6,000 Model S units in the fourth quarter and raised its total global deliveries outlook for 2013 to 21,500 from about 21,000 previously.

Abercrombie & Fitch /quotes/zigman/167627/delayed/quotes/nls/anf ANF -13.52%  said late Tuesday same-store sales plummeted 14% while total sales slid 12% to $1.03 billion in the third quarter. It also announced the closure of all stand-alone Gilly Hicks stores although the brand will continue to be sold online and at Hollister stores. Shares of Abercrombie & Fitch skidded over 6% in after-hours trading.

Humana /quotes/zigman/229688/delayed/quotes/nls/hum HUM +2.04%  is forecast to post third-quarter earnings of $2.15 a share, according to a consensus survey by FactSet.

Time Warner Inc. /quotes/zigman/528906/delayed/quotes/nls/twx TWX -0.79%  is projected to report third-quarter earnings of 89 cents a share.

Ralph Lauren Corp. /quotes/zigman/6233187/delayed/quotes/nls/rl RL +5.45%  is expected to post $2.20 a share in the third quarter.

Whole Foods Market Inc. /quotes/zigman/80039/delayed/quotes/nls/wfm WFM +1.22%  is likely to report fiscal fourth-quarter earnings of 31 cents a share. “Whole Foods remains well positioned to expand units, comparable stores sales and earnings at attractive rates capitalizing on the budding trends in health and wellness, in our view. While Whole Foods’ earnings power has consistently proven stronger than we had anticipated over the last several years, we believe the pace of upside will moderate,” Bob Summers, an analyst at Susquehanna, said in a report.
