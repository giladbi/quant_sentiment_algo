By Sue Chang, MarketWatch

SAN FRANCISCO (MarketWatch) — Among the companies whose shares are expected to see active trade in Wednesday’s session are General Motors Co., Facebook Inc., and Sprint Corp.

GM /quotes/zigman/1466682/delayed/quotes/nls/gm GM +2.06%  is projected to report third-quarter earnings of 94 cents a share, according to a consensus survey by FactSet.

Investors are expected to primarily focus on comments about the company’s capital allocation strategy and latest update on operations in Europe, James Albertine, an analyst at Stifel Nicolaus, said in a recent report. “Our sense is GM has many difficult, complex negotiations ahead as it relates production rationalization in the E.U. We also believe there is some confusion related to GM branding efforts (Chevrolet vs. Opel) as well as the status of existing partnerships [and] JVs,” he said.

Facebook /quotes/zigman/9962609/delayed/quotes/nls/fb FB -0.06%  is likely to post earnings of 19 cents a share in the third quarter. “We think 3Q results will further cement FB’s positioning in and growing share of digital advertising. We expect mobile advertising revenue to be up 15% sequentially and represent 40% of the mix. On the earnings call, we expect more color on future growth opportunities including Instagram and video ads,” Arvind Bhatia, an analyst at Sterne Agee, said in a note.

Sprint /quotes/zigman/18855261/delayed/quotes/nls/s S +1.14%  is forecast to post a loss of 23 cents a share in the third quarter. Wall Street is expected to focus on Sprint’s postpaid subscriber losses although analysts at Deutsche Bank believe the “churn,” or the attrition rate, is more important as it has greater impact on the company’s strategy.

Starbucks Corp. /quotes/zigman/20720/delayed/quotes/nls/sbux SBUX +2.69%  is expected to post fourth-quarter earnings of 59 cents a share. “We believe Starbucks has good demand momentum heading into the holidays and solid runway to use technology to sustain growth,” said James Faucette at Pacific Crest Securities. He reiterated the stock’s outperform rating on Tuesday.

Comcast Crop. /quotes/zigman/89307/delayed/quotes/nls/cmcsa CMCSA +1.99%   is likely to post earnings of 60 cents a share in the third quarter.

Kraft Foods Group Inc. /quotes/zigman/11825124/delayed/quotes/nls/krft KRFT -1.33%  is forecast to post third-quarter earnings of 68 cents a share.

Corning Inc. is expected to post third-quarter earnings of 32 cents a share.

PG&E Corp. /quotes/zigman/171662/delayed/quotes/nls/pcg PCG -0.67%  is projected to report third-quarter earnings of 79 cents a share.

Philips 66 /quotes/zigman/9483013/delayed/quotes/nls/psx PSX +2.38%  is forecast to report earnings of 93 cents a share in the third quarter.

Visa Inc. /quotes/zigman/502306/delayed/quotes/nls/v V +1.12%  is expected to post fourth-quarter earnings of $1.85 a share.

Expedia Inc. /quotes/zigman/7619798/delayed/quotes/nls/expe EXPE +1.65%  is likely to post third-quarter earnings of $1.36 a share. “Our checks point to relative strength in paid search on Expedia.com and Hotels.com in 3Q and signs of incremental progress on the TripAdvisor channel. With hotel trends relatively stable [quarter-on-quarter] across the U.S. and Europe, we believe it should translate into healthy double-digit growth in hotel room-nights and revenue,” Naved Khan, an analyst at Cantor Fitzgerald, said in a note. He has a buy rating on the stock.

Marriott International Inc. /quotes/zigman/23609459/delayed/quotes/nls/mar MAR +1.52%   is projected to report third-quarter earnings of 45 cents a share.

MetLife Inc. /quotes/zigman/252112/delayed/quotes/nls/met MET +5.36%  is forecast to earn $1.36 a share in the third quarter.

After Tuesday’s closing bell, LinkedIn Corp. /quotes/zigman/5131883/delayed/quotes/nls/lnkd LNKD +1.75%  said it swung to a loss of 3 cents a share in the third quarter from a profit of 2 cents a share a year earlier. Excluding items, the social networking company earned 39 cents a share. Shares fell 1.9% in after-hours trading.

Yelp Inc. /quotes/zigman/9021597/delayed/quotes/nls/yelp YELP +2.51%  also reported a third-quarter loss of 4 cents a share, wider than the 3-cent-a-share loss from a year ago. Analysts had forecast a loss of a penny a share in the quarter. Separately, Yelp said it is seeking to raise $250 million via an offering of common shares. It plans to use the proceeds for “working capital” and possibly to fund acquisitions. Shares slumped over 4% in after hours.

Electronic Arts Inc. /quotes/zigman/71356/delayed/quotes/nls/ea EA +1.46%  said second-quarter adjusted earnings more than doubled to 33 cents a share from 15 cents a share a year ago. Analysts had forecast the game publisher to report 12 cents a share. EA shares jumped 3% in late trading.

Take-Two Interactive Software Inc. /quotes/zigman/62747/delayed/quotes/nls/ttwo TTWO +0.64%  reported a loss of $1.40 a share in the fiscal second quarter. However, excluding items, the publisher of ”Grand Theft Auto V” earned $2.49 a share. Analysts had projected adjusted earnings of $1.72 cents a share. Shares of Take-Two gained more than 1% in after hours.
