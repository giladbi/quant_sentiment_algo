By Sue Chang, MarketWatch

SAN FRANCISCO (MarketWatch) — Among the shares expected to see active trade in Wednesday’s session are those of Yahoo Inc., Intel Corp., Bank of America Corp. and International Business Machines Corp.


Bloomberg
Enlarge Image

Yahoo /quotes/zigman/59898/quotes/nls/yhoo YHOO -0.35%  and Intel released quarterly results after Tuesday’s closing bell. Yahoo said its third-quarter profit fell to $296.7 million, or 28 cents a share, from $3.16 billion, or $2.64 a share, a year ago. On an adjusted basis, the Internet company earned 34 cents a share, slightly above the 33 cents a share forecast by analysts. Shares of Yahoo rose 1.7% in after hours.

Intel /quotes/zigman/20392/quotes/nls/intc INTC -0.27%  reported third-quarter earnings of $2.95 billion, or 58 cents a share, mostly unchanged from a year earlier. Although the results came in above Wall Street’s forecast of 53 cents a share, the stock fell 1.7% in after-hours trading after an initial gain.

On Wednesday, Bank of America /quotes/zigman/190927/quotes/nls/bac BAC 0.00%  is projected to report third-quarter profit of 19 cents a share, according to analysts at J.P. Morgan. They recently lowered their earnings-per-share estimate for Bank of America from 20 cents due to lower trading activity and a decline in mortgage-banking revenue.

IBM /quotes/zigman/230066/quotes/nls/ibm IBM +1.22% is projected to report earnings of $3.96 a share in the third quarter, according to Thomson Reuters. “We think conversion of IBM’s software pipeline was mixed, and hardware results will likely be weak once again. In conjunction with IBM’s depressed valuation, expectations appear low heading into results on Wednesday,” said Deutsche Bank analysts in a report.

PepsiCo Inc. /quotes/zigman/238082/quotes/nls/pep PEP +1.30%  is forecast to earn $1.17 a share in the third quarter, according to analysts in a Thomson Reuters poll.

EBay Inc. /quotes/zigman/76117/quotes/nls/ebay EBAY -0.21%  is expected to post third-quarter earnings of 63 cents a share, according to Thomson Reuters. Macro-economic headwinds in Europe and concerns over the U.S. government shutdown have limited the stock’s upside but did not significantly affect third-quarter e-commerce sales, Benchmark Research analyst Daniel Kurnos said in a note.

American Express Co. /quotes/zigman/217470/quotes/nls/axp AXP +0.58%  is likely to post earnings of $1.22 a share in the third quarter, according to a survey by Thomson Reuters.
